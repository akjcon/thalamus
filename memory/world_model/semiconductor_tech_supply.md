# Semiconductors & Technology Supply Chains
**As of: March 2, 2026**

## Key Chokepoints

### Taiwan (TSMC)
- TSMC produces ~90% of world's most advanced chips (sub-5nm)
- Any Taiwan Strait conflict = catastrophic global technology disruption
- TSMC building fabs in: Arizona (USA), Kumamoto (Japan), Dresden (Germany)
- Diversification takes years; Taiwan concentration remains dominant near-term

### ASML (Netherlands)
- Only manufacturer of EUV lithography machines (required for advanced chips)
- Netherlands controls export licenses under U.S. pressure
- China cannot obtain EUV machines; limits domestic chip advancement

### NVIDIA / Advanced AI Chips
- U.S. export controls blocking advanced AI chips (H100/H200) to China
- China developing domestic alternatives (Huawei Ascend, Cambricon) — 2–3 generations behind
- AI chip access = strategic military and economic advantage

## U.S. Export Controls (Current)
- Advanced logic chips: Blocked to China
- Advanced chip manufacturing equipment: Blocked (Netherlands, Japan coordinating)
- AI model weights and software: Under review
- Outbound investment in Chinese semiconductor sector: Restricted

## China's Response
- Massive domestic semiconductor investment (~$150B+ fund)
- SMIC advancing to 7nm (workarounds without EUV)
- Huawei re-emerging with domestically produced chips
- Timeline to competitive advanced chips: Estimated 5–10 years

## Other Critical Tech Supply Chains
- **Rare earths for chips/defense:** China ~70% refining (leverage point)
- **PCB and electronic components:** Heavy China/Taiwan concentration
- **Lithium batteries:** China dominant in refining and cell manufacturing

## Trajectory
⚠️ STRUCTURAL RISK — Taiwan concentration remains; export controls effective but porous; China advancing slowly

## Watch For
- Taiwan Strait military incidents
- ASML export license decisions
- TSMC Arizona/Japan fab ramp-up progress
- Chinese domestic chip advancement milestones
- New U.S. export control categories (AI software, cloud)
