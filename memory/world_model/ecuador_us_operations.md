## Situation Overview — CONFIRMED DETAILS (UPDATED)
- **U.S. role UPGRADED:** Joint operations officially launched March 3 — **confirmed joint raids, not merely advisory/intelligence role**
  - Prior characterization (advisory/intelligence/logistics) was understated
  - U.S. Special Forces conducting operations alongside Ecuadorian forces
- **SOUTHCOM General Donovan + SOCOM Rear Admiral Schafer visited Ecuador March 2** — met President Noboa and Minister of Defense immediately before operations launched
- **Eloy Alfaro Air Base (Manta) confirmed hosting U.S. Air Force elements since December 2025** — re-activation of former Forward Operating Base from early 2000s
- **Ecuador formally requested the U.S. presence:** Noboa ordered foreign ministry to seek agreements in February 2026
- SOFA covers temporary deployments
- **Constitutional ban on permanent foreign military bases upheld in November 2025 referendum** (>60% voted against permanent bases) — Manta presence is formally temporary; legal ambiguity remains
- Noboa government **weakened post-referendum**; active constitutional court conflict

## Atlantic Narco-Submarine Route — STRUCTURAL SHIFT CONFIRMED ⚠️ NEW
- **Atlantic narco-submarine route to Europe is confirmed structural, not experimental:**
  - Belgium/Spain/Netherlands = 75% of European cocaine seizures (EUDA data)
  - Vessels reaching Azores, Sierra Leone — **transoceanic capability demonstrated**
  - **Gulf Clan deploying unmanned narco-subs with Starlink antennas** — significant capability escalation from crewed vessels
- **Pacific interdiction pressure is documented driver of Atlantic pivot:**
  - 44 U.S. strikes on cartel infrastructure since September 2025 in Pacific theater
  - Interdiction success in Pacific is causing route displacement to Atlantic
  - Ecuador operations may accelerate this displacement dynamic
- **Implication:** Atlantic shipping lanes now have a documented narco-trafficking infrastructure overlay
  - Law enforcement is aware but not yet fully suppressing
  - Unmanned narco-sub capability raises detection challenges
  - **Not a supply chain threat per se** but relevant background for Atlantic maritime security
- **Compounding factor:** Ecuador operations targeting Pacific-side trafficking infrastructure → expected further cartel adaptation toward Atlantic routes
