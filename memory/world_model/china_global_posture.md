# China — Global Strategic Posture
**As of: March 2, 2026**

## Core Strategy
- Resist U.S. decoupling while maintaining export-led growth
- Expand strategic mineral leverage as trade war tool
- Support Russia indirectly (avoiding secondary sanctions)
- Assert Taiwan Strait and South China Sea dominance
- Expand Global South influence via BRI and development finance

## Key Dynamics

### Middle East Crisis — MAJOR NEW EXPOSURE
- China is the **largest single stakeholder** in Middle East energy stability:
  - **Iranian oil:** ~85%+ of Iranian exports go to China; now disrupted by Hormuz + conflict
  - **Qatar LNG:** Major Chinese customer; production halted
  - **Saudi/UAE oil:** China's primary non-Iran Gulf suppliers; infrastructure under attack
- China has **strong incentive to push for de-escalation** — Hormuz closure is an economic emergency for China
- **Confirmed response: muted verbal condemnation of strikes; no material support pledge for Iran**
  - Iran's 'protect Chinese citizens' statement to Wang Yi failed to draw China into protective role
  - Consistent with historical pattern: economic interests over ideological alignment when alignment cost is high
  - China's primary goal confirmed: Hormuz reopening, not Iran's military survival
- China likely to use back-channels with Iran (relationship) and U.S. (trade truce) to push ceasefire
- **Risk:** Being seen as pro-Iran backer creates complications with Gulf states
- **JKM (Asian LNG benchmark) spiking** — China facing simultaneous oil and gas import cost shock

### Power of Siberia 2 — ACCELERATING SIGNAL
- Qatar LNG disruption + JKM spike = China's incentive to accelerate Power of Siberia 2 negotiations with Russia is now acute
- Russia has strong incentive to complete deal (cash + strategic lock-in)
- **Track Power of Siberia 2 negotiation signals as real-time indicator of how long Beijing expects Qatar LNG outage to last**
  - Acceleration = China pricing in prolonged (months) supply shock
  - Stalling = China expects quick Hormuz resolution
- Mutual benefit at maximum; negotiations likely to accelerate rapidly

### Ammonia/Fertilizer Exposure
- China has domestic gas-based ammonia/fertilizer production; partially insulated from TTF spike
- But JKM spike propagates to Chinese industrial gas costs
- **Chinese urea export restriction decision is a critical binary** for global nitrogen markets:
  - Restrict exports (as China has done before for domestic food security) → global nitrogen shortage acute
  - Allow exports → partially cushions dual-channel disruption spike
- Decision expected within 2–3 weeks; watch as high-priority signal
- Emergency response options: Strategic LNG reserve deployment; accelerated pipeline gas imports

### CPEC / Pakistan-Afghanistan Exposure
- Pakistan direct strikes on Taliban positions in Afghanistan = new risk vector for CPEC infrastructure
- Chinese workers and investments in Pakistan already periodically targeted
- Afghanistan–Pakistan escalation adds threat vector beyond existing Baloch separatist risk
- **China likely to push for de-escalation** given CPEC exposure — potential mediator role
- Watch Chinese MFA statements on Pakistan-Afghanistan as proxy for CPEC threat assessment level

### Trade War
- Trade truce with USA (Oct 2025); reciprocal Trump–Xi visits planned
- Maintains ~70% control of critical mineral refining as structural leverage
- Middle East crisis may create **shared interest with U.S. in Hormuz resolution** → possible diplomatic coordination
- China has unusually strong reason to cooperate with U.S. on ceasefire diplomacy right now
- Rare earth export control deployment: **less likely while China needs U.S. cooperation on Hormuz**

### Taiwan
- Military pressure campaign continues (exercises, airspace incursions)
- No armed conflict as of March 2026
- U.S. attention consumed by Middle East; window of reduced U.S. focus technically opens
- **BUT:** China's own energy crisis from Hormuz creates strong disincentive for new military adventurism that would further destabilize energy markets or rupture U.S. diplomatic cooperation needed for Hormuz

### South China Sea
- Continued island fortification; friction with Philippines (U.S. treaty ally)
- Philippines–China confrontations at Scarborough Shoal and Second Thomas Shoal ongoing

### Russia Relationship
- "No limits" partnership maintained
- China buying Russian oil, gas; providing dual-use goods
- Russia benefits from energy price spike; China hurt by it — tension in alignment
- **Power of Siberia 2 acceleration: strongest shared interest in relationship right now**

### Myanmar
- Backing junta with drones and diplomatic support
- Priority: border stability + rare earth access

## Economic Pressures
- Property sector crisis ongoing (Evergrande aftermath)
- Youth unemployment elevated
- Deflationary pressure; domestic consumption weak
- **NEW: Energy import costs spiking — simultaneous oil and LNG shock = stagflationary pressure**
- Ammonia/fertilizer input costs rising via JKM transmission
- CPEC security costs potentially rising

## Trajectory
⚠️ ASSERTIVE but now managing acute energy supply crisis from Middle East — confirmed verbal-only Iran posture; strong incentive to use diplomatic leverage with both Iran and U.S. for Hormuz resolution; Power of Siberia 2 negotiations likely to accelerate significantly; urea export decision imminent

## Watch For
- **Chinese diplomatic intervention for Hormuz ceasefire** — back-channel with Iran AND with U.S.
- **Trump–Xi summit acceleration** — both sides now have strong shared interest (Hormuz)
- **Power of Siberia 2 negotiation signals** — acceleration = China expecting prolonged crisis
- **Chinese urea export restriction decision** (2–3 weeks) — critical binary for global nitrogen markets
- Chinese domestic energy emergency response measures (strategic LNG reserve deployment)
- Chinese MFA statements on Pakistan-Afghanistan = CPEC threat assessment proxy
- Taiwan Strait: counterbalanced by China's own energy emergency and need for U.S. Hormuz cooperation
- Chinese rare earth export control decisions (less likely while needing U.S. Hormuz cooperation)
- JKM price trajectory as Chinese gas cost indicator
- NK dialogue posture changes as axis-consolidation signal
