# Russia — Global Strategic Posture
**As of: March 2, 2026**

## Core Strategy
- Sustain war in Ukraine while seeking favorable negotiated outcome
- Evade Western sanctions via shadow fleet, third-country intermediaries
- Deepen Africa/Sahel presence via Wagner/Africa Corps
- Maintain China relationship as economic lifeline
- Use energy as geopolitical leverage where possible

## Ukraine War Economics
- War economy: ~7% of GDP on defense spending
- Oil revenues sustaining war despite sanctions (India/China/Türkiye buying)
- Shadow fleet enables continued crude exports
- Ruble volatile; inflation elevated
- Western technology imports via Armenia, Kyrgyzstan, UAE circumvention routes
- **NEW: Oil price spike from Middle East crisis is a significant windfall for Russia**

## Middle East Crisis — Russia's Position
- Russia **not directly involved** in Iran war
- **Beneficiary of energy price spike:** Higher oil revenues at exactly the moment it needs cash
- **Strategic opportunity:** Western attention diverted; Ukraine negotiations may stall favorably
- **Risk:** Iran (Russia's partner) is being militarily degraded; IRGC autonomous = unpredictable
- May use European energy desperation (Qatar LNG halt) to seek sanctions relief leverage
- Watching for any weakening of European will on Ukraine support

## Africa Expansion
- Wagner (rebranded Africa Corps post-Prigozhin) active in:
  - Mali, Burkina Faso, Niger (Sahel)
  - Libya, Sudan (RSF links), Central African Republic, Mozambique
- Payment in: cash, mining concessions, political influence
- Filling vacuum left by French withdrawal

## Energy Leverage
- Arctic LNG projects threatened by EU icebreaker sanctions
- Pipeline gas to Europe essentially terminated (Nord Stream sabotaged 2022)
- TurkStream still operational (limited)
- Russia pivoting gas exports to China via Power of Siberia
- **NEW: European energy emergency may create pressure to pause Russian LNG sanctions — Moscow aware**
