# Iran — Succession & Post-Khamenei Governance
**Status: 🔴 ACTIVE — Deep governance vacuum; contested power centers**
**As of: March 2, 2026**

## Constitutional Framework
- **Supreme Leader** is constitutionally required to be a senior Shiite cleric (Marja or near-equivalent)
- **Assembly of Experts:** 88-member clerical body; sole constitutional authority to elect new Supreme Leader
- Assembly must convene, deliberate, and vote — timeline: weeks to months under normal circumstances; wartime conditions add further uncertainty

## Interim Leadership Council (Confirmed March 1, 2026)
| Person | Role | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| Ali Larijani | SNSC (National Security Council) | De facto crisis manager; constitutionally ineligible for Supreme Leader (not a senior cleric) |
| Masoud Pezeshkian | President | Elected 2024; reformist-aligned; limited independent power |
| Gholam-Hossein Mohseni-Ejei | Judiciary Chief | Conservative; clerical credentials |

## Larijani — Key Profile
- **De facto power broker** for state survival / crisis management
- Long-serving SNSC chief and former parliament speaker
- **'Pragmatist'** in the sense of Iranian state interest; NOT a liberal reformer
  - Oversaw lethal crackdown on 2019 and 2022 protests
- **Constitutionally ineligible for Supreme Leader** — lacks required senior cleric status
- **U.S.-sanctioned January 15, 2026** for protest crackdown role
  - This sanction directly complicates any direct U.S.–Larijani back-channel
  - Oman intermediary channel may be required to work around this
- Likely to push for ceasefire / state survival track — but cannot order IRGC to stop

## Vahidi — IRGC Rival Power Center
- **Ahmad Vahidi named IRGC commander** post-Khamenei death
- Controls IRGC autonomous military operations (pre-arranged protocols)
- Potential rival to Larijani council for actual decision-making authority
- **Key question:** Do IRGC field commanders take orders from Vahidi or recognize the civilian council?
- IRGC institutional culture: prioritizes ideological mission and organizational survival over civilian political authority
- Vahidi's political/ceasefire orientation: **unknown — critical intelligence gap**

## The Core Power Contest
```
Layjani Council (SNSC/President/Judiciary)
    → Represents: state/political/diplomatic track
    → Incentive: ceasefire, state preservation, Oman channel
    → Constraint: cannot directly order IRGC operations; U.S. sanctions on Larijani

Vahidi / IRGC Command
    → Represents: autonomous military operations; pre-arranged protocols
    → Incentive: unclear; ideological mission; organizational survival
    → Constraint: facing air supremacy loss; ballistic missile inventory depleting
```
- This split explains why FM signals de-escalation simultaneously with continued IRGC strikes
- Resolution of this contest is the single most important variable for ceasefire viability

## Assembly of Experts — Supreme Leader Timeline
- 88-member clerical body; currently led by conservative clerics
- Must convene to elect new Supreme Leader
- **Candidates must be senior Shiite clerics** — disqualifies Larijani, Pezeshkian, most technocrats
- Potential clerical candidates: senior members of Assembly itself; names not yet surfaced publicly
- Wartime conditions complicate convening; Iranian territory under aerial interdiction
- **Timeline estimate:** Weeks to months minimum; potentially longer if war continues
- Until elected: Larijani council fills power vacuum by default, not by constitutional mandate

## NATO/Think Tank Assessment
- **Post-regime governance framework explicitly absent** — Iran had no formal succession plan for this scenario
- This is unprecedented in Islamic Republic history
- Risk: multiple competing power centers; no single authority capable of binding ceasefire commitments
- **Ceasefire agreements may be signed by council but not honored by IRGC** — this is the structural risk
