# Venezuela — U.S. Military Intervention & Post-Maduro Transition
**Status: POST-INTERVENTION — Transitional instability; Iran-Venezuela sanctions evasion axis confirmed active through late 2025**
**As of: March 3, 2026**

## Key Events
- **January 3, 2026:** U.S. military captured Nicolás Maduro in nighttime raid
- Maduro transported to New York to face criminal charges
- Trump promised U.S. oil/gas companies would return to invest in Venezuelan infrastructure
- Venezuelan crude sitting idle on tankers due to blockade; exports cratered

## Iran-Venezuela Sanctions Evasion — CONFIRMED OPERATIONAL (DISRUPTED)
- **DOJ civil forfeiture complaint (MT Skipper):** 1.8M barrels of PDVSA crude confirmed as primary source evidence that Iran-Venezuela sanctions evasion was **operationally active through at least November/December 2025**
- Post-Maduro capture: this axis is disrupted but not formally dismantled
- Transitional government's posture on continuing oil swap arrangements with Iran is unclear
- Iran's own energy capacity is now severely degraded by the regional war
- **Net assessment:** Venezuela-Iran oil swap axis was real, documented, and is now disrupted by both Maduro's capture and Iran's wartime condition

## Cuba — Cascading Consequences
- Cuba had relied heavily on Venezuelan oil at subsidized terms
- **U.S. fuel blockade of Venezuelan oil to Cuba now confirmed as active** — Cuba facing acute oil shortage
- Iran, Cuba's other potential supplier, is now in active war — supply constrained
- Venezuela's new transitional government unlikely to continue subsidized Cuba oil exports
- Cuba losing its two primary energy supporters simultaneously
- Cuba's internal stability increasingly at risk — watch as cascading consequence

## Current Risks
- **ELN** (Colombian rebel group; several thousand fighters in Venezuela) committed to attack foreign forces
- Political vacuum; fragmented government
- Military regime change more likely to trigger chaos + refugee flows than smooth transition
- Cuba, Russia, China lose key ally; potential spoiler role

## Latin American Axis — CONTRACTING
- **Bolivia:** Center-right Rodrigo Paz elected — ends Bolivia's Iran/Venezuela/Cuba alignment; reduces Iran's Latin American axis materially
- **Nicaragua:** Making concessions to Washington — cancelled Cuban visa-free travel (Feb 2026); weakening axis positioning
- **Venezuela:** Post-Maduro transitional government; prior Iran/Cuba alignment disrupted
- **Cuba:** Losing both Venezuela oil and Iran support simultaneously; isolated
- **Net assessment:** Iran's Latin American support axis is contracting, not expanding; Venezuela protests (if any) are largely symbolic given Maduro's capture and new government's U.S. realignment

## Commodities — OIL
- Venezuela holds world's **largest proven oil reserves** (Orinoco heavy crude)
- Current production fraction of capacity due to infrastructure decay + sanctions
- U.S. companies eyeing re-entry
- **PDVSA restart timeline:** Uncertain; heavy crude requires diluent imports + refinery investment
- Iran-Venezuela oil swap disrupted — reduces one evasion channel for both sides

## Trajectory
⚠️ HIGHLY UNCERTAIN — No functioning government; armed groups active; oil infrastructure degraded; but Latin American axis around Venezuela-Cuba-Iran is materially contracting; Cuba most acutely exposed to cascading consequences
