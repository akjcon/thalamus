# Syria — Sanctions Lifting & Reconstruction
**Status: SANCTIONS LARGELY LIFTED — Reconstruction opening**
**As of: March 2, 2026**

## Key Events
- Assad regime fell (late 2024)
- New government under **Ahmed al-Sharaa** (formerly HTS/Jabhat al-Nusra leader)
- **Nov 2025:** Al-Sharaa visited White House — landmark diplomatic normalization
- **July 2025:** U.S. comprehensive territory-wide sanctions formally terminated
- **2026 NDAA:** Repealed remaining broad-based secondary sanctions against Syria

## Remaining Complications
- Assad-era individuals still individually sanctioned
- Proximity to Russia/Iran creates compliance complexity for foreign businesses
- Israel continuing strikes on Syrian military infrastructure
- Sectarian tensions under new government (Alawite, Kurdish, Druze communities)
- HTS/al-Sharaa's Islamist background creates Western political hesitancy

## Opportunities
- Major reconstruction investment potential (infrastructure, energy, housing)
- European businesses exploring market entry
- Gulf states (UAE, Saudi Arabia, Qatar) investing
- Potential for energy corridor development

## Trajectory
🟢 DE-ESCALATING on sanctions / ⚠️ UNCERTAIN on political stability

## Watch For
- European and Gulf investment flows into Syria
- Israel–Syria relations and strike frequency
- Sectarian/Kurdish tensions under new government
- U.S. compliance guidance for businesses entering Syria
