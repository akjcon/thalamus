# U.S. Latin America Sanctions Complex
**Status: ESCALATING — Aggressive Trump posture**
**As of: March 2, 2026**

## Key Actions

### Venezuela
- Maduro captured Jan 3, 2026; facing U.S. criminal charges
- Sanctions regime in flux as U.S. manages post-intervention transition
- Oil company re-entry being planned

### Colombia
- **Oct 2025:** OFAC designated President Gustavo Petro under counternarcotics authorities
- Significant diplomatic rupture; Petro government hostile to U.S. intervention posture
- Colombian coal and agricultural trade affected by tensions

### Cuba
- Trump revoked prior administration's removal of Cuba from State Sponsor of Terrorism list
- Sanctions reimposed on entities linked to Cuban military, intelligence, security services
- Cuba loses Venezuela as primary economic backer

### Cartels / Gangs
- Trump administration designated numerous cartels and gangs as Foreign Terrorist Organizations (FTOs)
- Includes Mexican cartels, MS-13, Tren de Aragua, and others
- Enables broader financial sanctions + potential military action authority

### Nicaragua
- Ortega government under continued sanctions pressure

## Commodities Affected
- Venezuelan oil (world's largest reserves; production recovering)
- Colombian coal and emeralds
- Agricultural trade flows across the region
- Drug trade logistics (indirect commodity markets)

## Trajectory
🔴 ESCALATING — Most aggressive U.S. posture in Latin America in decades

## Watch For
- Colombia's response to Petro designation; trade retaliation risk
- Cuba's internal stability without Venezuelan support
- Cartel FTO designation legal/operational consequences
- Venezuela oil production restart pace
