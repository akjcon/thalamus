# U.S. Latin America Sanctions Complex
**Status: ESCALATING — Aggressive Trump posture; Latin American axis contracting**
**As of: March 3, 2026**

## Key Actions

### Venezuela
- Maduro captured Jan 3, 2026; facing U.S. criminal charges
- Sanctions regime in flux as U.S. manages post-intervention transition
- Oil company re-entry being planned
- **Iran-Venezuela oil swap confirmed operationally active through at least Nov/Dec 2025** (DOJ civil forfeiture complaint, MT Skipper, 1.8M barrels PDVSA crude) — now disrupted by Maduro capture and Iran's wartime condition

### Colombia
- **Oct 2025:** OFAC designated President Gustavo Petro under counternarcotics authorities
- Significant diplomatic rupture; Petro government hostile to U.S. intervention posture
- Colombian coal and agricultural trade affected by tensions

### Cuba
- Trump revoked prior administration's removal of Cuba from State Sponsor of Terrorism list
- Sanctions reimposed on entities linked to Cuban military, intelligence, security services
- **Cuba now facing acute oil shortage:** Venezuelan oil blockade confirmed active; Iran (other supplier) in active war and supply-constrained; Cuba losing both major energy patrons simultaneously
- Cuba's internal stability increasingly at risk as energy situation deteriorates

### Bolivia
- **Center-right Rodrigo Paz elected** — ends Bolivia's alignment with Iran/Venezuela/Cuba axis
- Significant axis contraction; Bolivia was one of the last remaining Latin American members of the Venezuela-Cuba-Iran bloc
- Paz expected to normalize relations with U.S.

### Nicaragua
- **Making concessions to Washington:** Cancelled Cuban visa-free travel (Feb 2026)
- Weakening its prior ideological alignment posture
- Ortega government bending under U.S. economic pressure

### Cartels / Gangs
- Trump administration designated numerous cartels and gangs as Foreign Terrorist Organizations (FTOs)
- Includes Mexican cartels, MS-13, Tren de Aragua, and others
- Enables broader financial sanctions + potential military action authority

## Latin American Axis Assessment
- Iran's Latin American support axis is **contracting, not expanding**:
  - Venezuela: post-Maduro; prior Iran alignment disrupted
  - Cuba: isolated; losing both energy patrons
  - Bolivia: now center-right; axis shift
  - Nicaragua: making Washington concessions
- **Venezuela protests** (if occurring): largely symbolic given Maduro's capture and political realignment under transitional government
- Russia and China maintain economic ties but cannot fill the political vacuum rapidly

## Commodities Affected
- Venezuelan oil (world's largest reserves; production recovering slowly)
- Colombian coal and emeralds
- Agricultural trade flows across the region
- Drug trade logistics (indirect commodity markets)

## Trajectory
🔴 ESCALATING on U.S. posture; but Latin American Iran-Venezuela-Cuba axis is contracting materially; Cuba most acutely exposed

## Watch For
- Cuba internal stability under energy shortage conditions
- Bolivia under Paz — follow-through on realignment
- Nicaragua — pace of further concessions to Washington
- Colombia's response to Petro designation; trade retaliation risk
- Cartel FTO designation legal/operational consequences
- Venezuela oil production restart pace under transitional government
- Russia/China economic engagement with Venezuela transitional government to preserve influence
