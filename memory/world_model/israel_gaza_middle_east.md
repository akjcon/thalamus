## Gaza

## Gaza Crossings — BLOCKADE TIED TO IRAN WAR DURATION ⚠️
- **COGAT statement (March 1, 2026):** Crossing closures explicitly linked to Iran war operations
- **Complete closure since March 2, 2025** — longest in blockade history
- Aid organizations including **World Central Kitchen running out of food within days**
- **Pattern documented (Oct 2025–Feb 2026):** Only 43% of allocated aid trucks entered
- **37 aid groups formally banned** from operating in Gaza
- **UNRWA blocked from bringing any assistance since March 2, 2025**
- **ICJ advisory opinion (Oct 2025):** Israel's obstruction of UNRWA violates international law
- **~$50M in goods stockpiled** at crossings/warehouses; unable to enter
- **Key implication:** Gaza crossing reopening now tied to Iran war ceasefire — no resolution until broader conflict ends; humanitarian collapse timeline extends with Iran war duration

## Lebanon — HEZBOLLAH IN OPEN WAR
- **Lebanon ceasefire confirmed OVER as of March 2, 2026** (U.S. confirmed)
- **Hezbollah declared 'open war'** — March 3, 2026
- **Hezbollah currently targeting:** Ramat David airbase, Meron monitoring base, Camp Yitzhak — military target pattern
- Israeli strikes on **Beirut** resumed
- IDF issued **evacuation orders for 50 villages** in southern Lebanon and Beqaa Valley
- **31,000+ newly displaced Lebanese** as of March 3; **11,000/day crossing into Syria** — acute humanitarian flow
- **Lebanese PM Nawaf Salam formally banned Hezbollah military activities, ordered arrests** — unprecedented state action; operationally irrelevant short-term but significant for post-conflict political architecture
- Hezbollah leader Qassem **marked for elimination** by Israel's defense minister
- IDF warned of **'long campaign'**
- **Lebanon parliamentary elections (May 2026):** Now in extreme jeopardy

## Hezbollah — Force Assessment
- **Confirmed force size:** 40,000–50,000 active combatants + 30,000–50,000 reservists
- **CRITICAL CAVEAT:** Hezbollah is entering open war in a **degraded state** — heavy missile inventory heavily depleted during 2024 conflict; 'open war' declaration may exceed current capability
- **Restraint mechanism clarified — now eliminated:**
  - Pre-March 2 restraint was explicitly contingent on **Supreme Leader (Khamenei) authorization threshold**
  - Khamenei's death was that authorization threshold
  - **No future civilian Iranian council can credibly restrain Hezbollah via the same mechanism**
  - Larijani council cannot substitute for Khamenei-level authority over Hezbollah
  - **Who can authorize Hezbollah stand-down is now an open question** — this is a key ceasefire obstacle
- **Implication for ceasefire:** Pre-war Oman deal terms required 'freezing Hezbollah'; Hezbollah is now in open war and lacks a Khamenei-equivalent to authorize stand-down; this term is now structurally harder to satisfy than when it was nearly agreed
