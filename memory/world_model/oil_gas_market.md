## Refined Products — CRITICAL: MORE DISRUPTED THAN CRUDE
- **Ras Tanura refinery CONFIRMED shutdown:** 550,000 b/d of **refined products** (diesel, gasoline, jet fuel, naphtha) offline
- **Crude price is NOT the right indicator** for markets with limited domestic refining — refined product availability is the acute shortage
- **Crack spreads** (diesel, gasoline, jet fuel premiums over crude): Watch as the leading indicator of downstream economic pain; should widen materially and sustainably
- **Most exposed markets (limited domestic refining):**
  - **Pakistan:** Facing acute diesel/gasoline shortage independent of crude price; combined with refined product cutoff and energy import cost
  - **Sri Lanka:** Limited domestic refining + goods import disruption = double shock
  - **East Africa:** Diesel/gasoline imports primarily from Gulf refineries
  - Gulf regional markets dependent on Saudi refined product exports
- Restart timeline for Ras Tanura: unknown; physical infrastructure damage assessment ongoing
- **Petrochemical feedstock — undertracked secondary shock:**
  - Naphtha (from Ras Tanura) is primary feedstock for plastics, synthetic fibers, chemical intermediates
  - Naphtha offline → European and Asian petrochemical margins compressed
  - Ethylene, propylene, polyolefin production costs rising
  - Watch European/Asian petrochemical company margin guidance as lagging indicator
