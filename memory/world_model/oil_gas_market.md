## LNG Market — ACUTE CRISIS
- Qatar halted: ~77 mt/year (~20% global supply) offline
- **Physical restart minimum: 3–6 weeks** — liquefaction train recommissioning, safety inspections; floors the duration of European gas supply shock regardless of ceasefire timing
- **TTF confirmed at €39.14/MWh** — above 2021–2022 nitrogen curtailment threshold
- **TTF–Henry Hub spread:** Core structural variable
  - Wide spread = U.S. LNG export arbitrage profitable; U.S. producers benefit
  - Drives forward curve expectations for European gas; signals duration of crisis
  - Also signals European nitrogen fertilizer curtailment risk (gas-intensive production)
- **Europe:** Emergency scenario confirmed; 2022-style crisis re-emerged
  - No immediate substitute at Qatar's volume
  - U.S. LNG terminals at capacity; cannot compensate at scale immediately
- **Japan/South Korea:** Major Qatar customers; acute disruption
- **China:** Qatar customer + losing Iranian oil access; JKM spike hitting simultaneously
