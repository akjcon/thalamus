## Refined Products — CRITICAL: MORE DISRUPTED THAN CRUDE
- **Ras Tanura refinery CONFIRMED shutdown:** 550,000 b/d of **refined products** (diesel, gasoline, jet fuel, naphtha) offline
- **Crude price is NOT the right indicator** for markets with limited domestic refining — refined product availability is the acute shortage
- **Two distinct product shocks — track separately:**

### Shock A: Diesel / Gasoline / Jet Fuel Shortage
- Affects transportation, agriculture logistics, power generation in fuel-import-dependent nations
- **Crack spreads** (diesel, gasoline, jet fuel premiums over crude): Watch as the leading indicator; should widen materially and sustainably
- **Most exposed:** Pakistan, Sri Lanka, East Africa (limited domestic refining; imports primarily from Gulf refineries)

### Shock B: Naphtha / Petrochemical Feedstock Shortage — DISTINCT SECONDARY SHOCK
- Naphtha from Ras Tanura is primary feedstock for plastics, synthetic fibers, chemical intermediates, packaging
- **Distinct downstream industries** from fuel shock — affects European/Asian petrochemical companies, NOT the same companies or supply chains as the nitrogen/fertilizer cascade
- Ethylene, propylene, polyolefin production costs rising
- European petrochemical companies face simultaneously: higher naphtha feedstock costs + higher energy costs (TTF)
- **This is a separate earnings story** from fertilizer producers — different companies, different timing, different downstream exposure:
  - **LyondellBasell** (plastics/polyolefins)
  - **BASF** (chemicals; dual exposure: both fertilizer and petrochemical)
  - **Covestro** (specialty chemicals)
  - **Ineos** (polyolefins, chemical intermediates)
  - **Borealis** (polyolefins)
- Watch for **earnings pre-announcements and margin guidance warnings** from these companies as lagging indicators
- **Undertracked by markets** — naphtha/petrochemical channel not receiving proportionate attention vs. crude
- Timeline to appear in public data: 4–6 weeks (next quarterly guidance season)
