# Asia-Pacific — Geopolitical Overview
**As of: March 3, 2026 (UPDATED)**

## Taiwan Strait
- No armed conflict as of March 2026
- China continuing military pressure: exercises, airspace incursions, gray zone operations
- TSMC remains world's most critical semiconductor manufacturer
- U.S. commitment: strategic ambiguity maintained; arms sales continuing
- Taiwan elections (Jan 2024) returned DPP — Beijing hostile to outcome
- China's record oil stockpile build flagged as potential Taiwan conflict preparation indicator — but current energy crisis paradoxically creates disincentive for new military adventurism

## South China Sea
- China asserting control: island fortification, coast guard confrontations
- **Philippines:** Regular confrontations at Scarborough Shoal and Second Thomas Shoal; U.S. treaty ally
- **Vietnam:** Resisting Chinese pressure; pragmatic engagement
- ASEAN: Unable to form unified response

## Korean Peninsula
- North Korea: continued weapons development; testing ongoing
- **Kim personally oversaw cruise missile test from new naval destroyer (March 2026)** — interpret as domestic legitimacy signaling timed to reduced global attention on Korean peninsula
  - More operationally significant signals to watch: investments in hardened/underground assets or faster-launch systems — those would indicate NK drawing lessons from Iran decapitation strikes
  - Submarine capability tests or hardened-facility capability demonstrations would be more meaningful escalation signals than surface-ship tests
- North Korea supplying Russia with artillery shells and ammunition (Ukraine conflict)
- Kim–Putin relationship deepening
- **NK reducing dialogue openness** following Iran strikes — watch as indicator of Russia-China-NK axis consolidation
  - Possible interpretation: NK observing Iran outcome; hardening posture against preemptive strike risk
  - Not yet confirmed as coordinated axis response but worth tracking as pattern
- South Korea: domestic political instability (Yoon impeachment proceedings)
- U.S.–South Korea alliance: reaffirmed but cost-sharing tensions

## Japan
- Significant defense spending increase (2% GDP target reached)
- Deepening U.S.–Japan security cooperation
- Historical tensions with China/Korea managed
- Key TSMC fab being built in Kumamoto (supply chain diversification)
- Acute LNG disruption from Qatar halt — Japan is one of world's largest LNG importers

## India
- Post-Pakistan ceasefire; managing multiple borders (China, Pakistan)
- Buying Russian oil; strategic autonomy maintained
- Quad member; deepening U.S. security ties
- World's most populous country; growing economic power
- Pharmaceuticals: largest global generic drug exporter (supply chain risk if escalation; DXB air cargo disruption hitting India pharma exports NOW)

## Southeast Asia
- Myanmar civil war destabilizing; China managing junta
- ASEAN cohesion strained on Myanmar policy
- Indonesia: neutral; growing economy; nickel major producer
- Vietnam: key manufacturing alternative to China (beneficiary of trade war)

## Trajectory
⚠️ MULTIPLE STRUCTURAL TENSIONS — Taiwan and South China Sea as highest systemic risks; NK testing timed to reduced global attention on peninsula; Japan/South Korea facing acute LNG supply shock from Qatar halt; U.S. attention bandwidth consumed by Middle East reduces Asia-Pacific deterrence posture

## Watch For
- **North Korea hardened-facility or submarine capability tests** — more significant than surface-ship tests; would indicate NK drawing operational lessons from Iran decapitation (hardening against preemptive strike risk)
- **NK dialogue posture changes** as Russia-China-NK axis consolidation indicator
- Taiwan Strait military exercises/incidents (reduced U.S. attention = increased risk window)
- Philippines–China confrontation escalation
- South Korea political stability
- Myanmar junta territorial losses
- Japan/South Korea emergency LNG procurement response to Qatar halt
