# Sahel / West Africa — Jihadist Insurgency & Coup States
**Status: ESCALATING**
**As of: March 2, 2026**

## Current Situation
- Mali, Burkina Faso, Niger: all suffered military coups in past 5 years
- Juntas partnered with Wagner Group (Russia) to fight jihadists — ineffective
- Since September 2025: JNIM militants imposing partial blockade on Bamako (Mali's capital)
  - Goal: squeeze military authorities, consolidate rural control; not yet attempting city seizure
- Risk of regime collapse growing in Mali and Burkina Faso
- All three juntas expelled French and U.S. forces; pivoted to Russia

## Jihadist Groups
- **JNIM** (Jamaat Nusrat al-Islam wal-Muslimin) — al-Qaeda affiliate; dominant in Mali/Burkina
- **ISIS Sahel** — competing with JNIM; expanding in Niger/Nigeria border

## Spillover Risk
- Experts warn of expansion into: Ghana, Côte d'Ivoire, Benin
- Coastal West African states increasingly targeted by probe attacks

## External Actors
- **Russia/Wagner:** Primary security partners for juntas; not stabilizing
- **France:** Expelled from all three countries
- **USA:** Reduced presence; Niger expelled U.S. forces from Agadez drone base
- **ECOWAS:** Increasingly ineffective; Alliance of Sahel States (AES) formed as rival bloc

## Commodities Affected
- **Uranium:** Niger = significant producer; French nuclear supply chains disrupted
- **Gold:** Mali and Burkina Faso = major producers; mining areas contested
- **Agriculture:** Regional food security severely threatened; humanitarian crisis

## Trajectory
🔴 ESCALATING — Bamako blockade signals new phase; coastal spillover risk rising

## Watch For
- Whether Bamako negotiates or is encircled
- Spillover attacks in Ghana, Côte d'Ivoire, Benin
- Wagner/Russia entrenchment and resource extraction deals
- Niger uranium export disruption to France
