# Critical Minerals — Global Supply Chains
**As of: March 2, 2026**

## China's Dominance
- China dominates refining of **19 of 20 key strategic minerals** (IEA data)
- Average refining market share: ~70%
- This is the primary structural leverage China holds in U.S.–China trade war

## Key Minerals & Conflict Zones

| Mineral | Primary Source | Conflict Exposure | Use Case |
|---|---|---|---|
| Cobalt | DRC (~70% global) | M23/Rwanda conflict | EV batteries |
| Coltan/Tantalum | DRC | M23/Rwanda conflict | Electronics capacitors |
| Heavy rare earths (Tb, Dy) | Myanmar | Civil war / junta control | Magnets, defense |
| Light rare earths (Nd, Pr) | China dominant | Trade war | EV motors, wind turbines |
| Uranium | Niger | Sahel jihadist pressure | Nuclear energy |
| Gold | Mali, Burkina Faso, Sudan, DRC | Multiple conflicts | Reserve asset, electronics |
| Lithium | Chile, Australia, Argentina | Low conflict | EV batteries |
| Nickel | Indonesia, Philippines | Low conflict | EV batteries, stainless steel |

## Western Response Initiatives
- U.S.–DRC/Rwanda minerals deal (Trump brokered; finalization pending)
- EU Critical Raw Materials Act (stockpiling, diversification mandates)
- U.S. Mineral Security Partnership (MSP) — allied nations coordination
- Australia, Canada expanding production as alternatives to China

## Key Risks
- DRC cobalt: peace deal fragile; M23 controls mining areas
- Myanmar rare earths: civil war disrupting extraction and export
- Niger uranium: jihadist pressure + junta expulsion of French companies
- China export control deployment: China has used/threatened rare earth export bans as retaliation

## Trajectory
⚠️ HIGH RISK — Multiple conflict zones overlap with critical mineral production; China refining dominance persists medium-term

## Watch For
- Chinese rare earth export control announcements
- DRC minerals deal finalization
- New mine development timelines outside China
- Myanmar rare earth export data anomalies
