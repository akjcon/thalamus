# Critical Minerals — Global Supply Chains
**As of: March 3, 2026 (UPDATED)**

## China's Dominance
- China dominates refining of **19 of 20 key strategic minerals** (IEA data)
- Average refining market share: ~70%
- This is the primary structural leverage China holds in U.S.–China trade war

## DRC Cobalt — CRITICAL UPDATE: Export Ban & Price Spike ⚠️
- **DRC enacted a cobalt export ban in June 2025** — most significant cobalt supply event of the decade
- **Cobalt price doubled from ~$10/lb → ~$25/lb** following ban
- **KEY UNKNOWN — HIGH PRIORITY RESEARCH QUESTION:** Whether the ban has been fully lifted, partially modified, or remains in force is unconfirmed
  - If lifted: Rapid price reversal possible; battery supply chain implications
  - If sustained/modified: Price floor maintained; EV battery cost structure implications
  - **Monitor:** DRC Ministry of Mines statements and cobalt spot price trajectory as proxies
- **Rubaya (coltan) — M23 control:** M23 is systematically taxing Rubaya coltan operations at ~$800K/month; Rwanda's coltan exports increased ~213% (consistent with M23 diverting Rubaya production through Rwanda)
- **DRC-U.S. minerals deal gap:** Deal offers Rubaya coltan as 'conflict-free' tantalum — aspirational; M23 controls the ground; implementation requires security breakthrough that doesn't exist
  - Flag for any U.S. investor: diplomatic ambition vs. M23 operational reality is an unbridged gap

## DRC Cobalt Export Ban — Market Implications
- **EV battery manufacturers (Tesla, BYD, CATL, Samsung SDI):** Direct cost impact if sustained at $25/lb vs. $10/lb baseline
- **Cobalt substitution timeline:** Battery chemistry shifting toward lower-cobalt (LFP) and cobalt-free architectures — accelerated by supply shock
- **Western battery supply chain:** Heavily DRC-dependent for cobalt; no rapid substitute at volume
- **Watch:** LME cobalt price as ban-status indicator; gap between $10 and $25/lb reflects supply constraint; any rapid reversal = ban eased

## Western Response Initiatives

- U.S. Mineral Security Partnership (MSP) — allied nations coordination
