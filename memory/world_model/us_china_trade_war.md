# U.S.–China Trade War & Technology Restrictions
**Status: SHORT TRUCE — Structural competition ongoing**
**As of: March 2, 2026**

## Current State
- Trade war truce agreed: **October 2025**
- Trump and Xi planning reciprocal visits in 2026 to clarify trajectory
- Truce is fragile; structural competition (tech, military, critical minerals) unresolved

## Tariff Legal Uncertainty
- U.S. Supreme Court struck down Trump's use of IEEPA to implement tariffs
- Administration pursuing "Plan B": alternative trade law sections, time-limited and product-specific levies
- USMCA review scheduled **July 2026** — China provisions will be contested

## Technology Restrictions (Beyond Tariffs)
- New regulatory regimes on U.S. **outbound investment** involving China
- **Cross-border data flow** restrictions for China and "countries of concern"
- Export controls intensified in late 2025 (advanced chips, semiconductor equipment)
- China dominant in **19 of 20 key strategic minerals** (avg. ~70% market share in refining) — structural leverage

## Key Flashpoints
- Semiconductors / advanced chips (TSMC, NVIDIA, ASML)
- Rare earth and critical mineral export controls (China's retaliation tool)
- EV batteries and solar panels
- Steel and aluminum
- TikTok / data / digital economy

## Key Actors
| Actor | Position |
|---|---|
| USA | Tech decoupling, tariffs, investment restrictions |
| China | Mineral leverage, market access retaliation |
| EU | Caught between; own tariffs on Chinese EVs |
| Japan/South Korea | Allied but economically exposed to China |
| Taiwan | Semiconductor chokepoint |

## Trajectory
⚠️ UNSTABLE — Truce holds nominally; legal uncertainty on tariffs; minerals leverage asymmetry favors China short-term

## Watch For
- Trump–Xi summit date and outcome
- USMCA review (July 2026) China provisions
- Supreme Court final tariff authority ruling
- Chinese rare earth export controls deployment
- Semiconductor export control escalation
