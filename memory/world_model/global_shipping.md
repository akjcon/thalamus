# Global Shipping — Disruptions & Routes
**Status: 🔴 DUAL CHOKEPOINT CRISIS — Physical disruption confirmed; 150 ships stranded**
**As of: March 2, 2026**

## CRITICAL SITUATION: Physical Hormuz Disruption Confirmed
- **Three tankers confirmed damaged** near Hormuz as of March 2
- **~150 ships stranded near Hormuz** — physical disruption beyond declaration
- War risk insurance for Gulf zone making some Gulf crude **economically unliftable**
- Both major energy/trade chokepoints simultaneously under acute threat — no modern precedent

## Red Sea / Bab el-Mandeb (🔴 ACTIVE ATTACKS)
- Houthis resumed attacks on U.S. and Israeli-flagged ships
- Triggered by Iran regional war (replaces Gaza ceasefire as justification)
- Cape of Good Hope rerouting resuming (+10–14 days, major fuel premium)
- Brief reopening (Dec 2025 — Maersk transit) fully reversed

## Strait of Hormuz (🔴 CLOSURE DECLARED — PHYSICALLY DISRUPTED)
- IRGC declared Hormuz closed
- U.S. carrier groups present and contesting closure
- **Confirmed:** 3 tankers damaged, ~150 ships stranded
- ~20% of global oil, significant LNG, petrochemicals transit daily
- **Saudi East-West pipeline to Yanbu:** Only partial Hormuz bypass (~5 mb/d capacity)
  - **COMPROMISED:** Red Sea also under Houthi attack — bypass route not viable as safe alternative
  - Saudi Arabia's largest domestic refinery also shuttered by drone strike

## Consequence: No Good Alternative Routes
- Red Sea blocked → Cape of Good Hope (+10–14 days)
- Hormuz blocked → No alternative for Gulf oil exports at volume
- Saudi EW pipeline to Yanbu → normally partial relief but Red Sea also compromised
- **Simultaneous blockage = Gulf states effectively cannot export oil/LNG at normal volumes by any sea route**

## Atlantic-Basin Crude Routing Emergence — STRUCTURAL SHIFT
- Gulf crude supply disruption redirecting Asian buyer attention to Atlantic-basin alternatives
- **West Africa (Nigeria, Angola)** and **U.S. Gulf (WTI)** grades becoming premium alternatives
- **VLCC ton-mile demand** on West Africa–Asia and U.S. Gulf–Asia routes spiking
  - Key metric to watch as structural demand signal
  - Rising VLCC charter rates on these routes = confirmation of route shift
- This reshapes tanker market fundamentally if Hormuz closure persists >2 weeks
- Atlantic-basin is now the de facto default for non-sanctioned Asian crude buyers

## War Risk Insurance — Economic Disruption Layer
- Lloyd's Gulf zone pricing spiking to extreme levels
- Insurance cost adding **$1–3/bbl equivalent** to Gulf crude transport cost
- Some Gulf crude cargoes economically unliftable at current insurance rates
- Rising premiums make compliant fleet relatively more competitive vs. shadow fleet
- **Lloyd's Gulf zone pricing = key market indicator of crisis severity assessment**

## Other Ongoing Disruptions

### Panama Canal
- 2024: Drought restrictions; 2025–2026 partially recovered
- Now a critical alternative if other routes fail; **under surge pressure from rerouting demand**
- U.S.–Panama tensions (Trump rhetoric) — soft risk

### Shadow Fleet
- ~600+ vessels; carrying Russian, Iranian, Venezuelan oil
- Iranian shadow fleet: irrelevant during Hormuz closure (can't exit)
- Russian shadow fleet: continues operating; not directly affected

## Shipping Rate Environment
- Container rates: Spiking as Red Sea reopening reverses
- Tanker rates: **Extreme spike** — Hormuz + Red Sea simultaneously
- War risk insurance: Extreme premium spikes; Gulf waters effectively uninsurable at normal rates
- Dry bulk: Elevated; commodity disruptions rippling through
