# Democratic Republic of Congo — Eastern DRC Crisis
**Status: ACTIVE — Nominal peace deal, implementation uncertain**
**As of: March 2, 2026**

## Current Situation
- Rwanda (via M23 proxies) has effectively annexed North and South Kivu provinces
- DRC–Rwanda peace deal signed in Washington, brokered by Trump
  - Terms: permanent ceasefire, disarmament of non-state forces, economic framework
  - Implementation: highly uncertain; violence continues
- U.S. announced bilateral agreements with DRC and Rwanda for natural resource purchases

## Key Actors
| Actor | Role |
|---|---|
| DRC government (Kinshasa) | Defending territorial integrity |
| M23 rebels | Rwanda-backed; controlling eastern territory |
| Rwanda (Kagame) | De facto annexing Kivus via proxy |
| Uganda | Regional actor with economic interests |
| USA | Deal broker; minerals buyer |
| Qatar | Mediator role |
| Burundi | Troops supporting DRC |
| UN | MONUSCO mission (diminished) |

## Commodities — CRITICAL
- **Cobalt:** DRC = ~70% of global production; essential for EV batteries
- **Coltan (Tantalum):** Electronics, capacitors
- **Gold:** Significant artisanal and industrial production
- **Tin:** Electronics solder
- Control of these minerals is the core prize for all armed actors

## Trajectory
⚠️ UNSTABLE — Peace deal signed but unimplemented; mineral access driving conflict

## Watch For
- U.S. minerals deal finalization with DRC/Rwanda
- Ceasefire monitoring force deployment
- M23 territorial consolidation vs. rollback
- Impact on global cobalt prices and EV supply chains
