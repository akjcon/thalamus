## East Africa — COMPOUNDING DOUBLE SHOCK FROM GULF CRISIS ⚠️
- East Africa is facing **two simultaneous shocks from the Gulf war** that are not being priced together by markets:
  1. **Consumer goods shortage** via Jebel Ali paralysis — critically reliant on Dubai as primary import transshipment hub; no viable rapid alternative; emergence timeline 4–6 weeks from crisis onset
  2. **Refined fuel shortage** via Ras Tanura offline (550,000 b/d) — limited domestic refining capacity; imports primarily from Gulf refineries; emergence timeline potentially faster for nations with no strategic reserves
- **Combined severity is multiplicative, not additive:** Fuel shortage degrades logistics and distribution capacity at exactly the moment goods shortages require adaptive responses
- **Most exposed countries:** Kenya, Tanzania, Uganda, Ethiopia
- **Also affected:** Diaspora remittance flows disrupted; air freight (cut flowers, fresh produce exports via DXB) disrupted immediately
- See: [consumer_goods_supply_chain.md](consumer_goods_supply_chain.md)

## UNHCR Funding Emergency — COMPOUNDING REGIONAL INSTABILITY ⚠️ SEVERITY UPGRADED
- **UNHCR at 15% of 2026 funding** ($454.2M needed; ~$68M received) — but the situation is structurally worse than this figure implies:
  - UNHCR's actual 2026 budget is **$8.5B** (already 20% below 2025 needs-based estimate)
  - **U.S. was 40% of UNHCR total funding** — its withdrawal is structural, not a temporary gap
  - **$1.4B in programs stopped/suspended** including **60% cuts in Sudan, Myanmar, Afghanistan**
  - **5,000 UNHCR staff positions cut** (one-third of global workforce)
  - **Sudan famine acceleration now has NO international monitoring/response infrastructure** at prior scale
- Fresh **Lebanon displacement wave** (11,000/day crossing into Syria) is hitting at the same time as:
  - Chronic Syria/Palestinian refugee caseloads
  - Sudan famine and displacement
  - DRC displacement
- **African refugee situations (Sudan, DRC, Sahel) competing for the same structurally underfunded UNHCR envelope** as new Lebanon/Middle East emergency
- **Jordan:** Mafraq/Irbid refugee infrastructure under strain from Lebanon wave
- **Secondary instability risk:** Underfunded responses to African displacement increase fragility in host communities (Uganda, Chad, Ethiopia, Kenya)
- **Syria reconstruction timeline extended** — 1.07M returnees now in country hosting new Lebanese inflow; humanitarian resources stretched
- **Sudan famine force multiplier:** No UNHCR monitoring capacity = no early warning, no aid coordination, no data for donor appeals — instability in Chad, Ethiopia, Egypt accelerates without buffer

## Egypt — EMERGING SECONDARY INSTABILITY RISK ⚠️ NEW
- Egypt faces a slow-moving but compounding fiscal stress from Gulf war:
  1. **Suez Canal revenue collapsed** — dual chokepoint (Hormuz + Red Sea) means Gulf trade volumes cratered; Suez is feeder to Red Sea; revenue at risk
  2. **Wheat import costs elevated** — Egypt is world's largest wheat importer; food price inflation risk
  3. **Potential Sudan refugee surge** — if Sudan famine accelerates without UNHCR buffer, Egypt faces refugee influx from its southern border
- Egypt's IMF program ($8B+) and FX reserves are buffer — but margin is limited
- **Not an imminent crisis** but watch as slow-accumulating pressure vector
