## East Africa — COMPOUNDING DOUBLE SHOCK FROM GULF CRISIS ⚠️
- East Africa is facing **two simultaneous shocks from the Gulf war** that are not being priced together by markets:
  1. **Consumer goods shortage** via Jebel Ali paralysis — critically reliant on Dubai as primary import transshipment hub; no viable rapid alternative; emergence timeline 4–6 weeks from crisis onset
  2. **Refined fuel shortage** via Ras Tanura offline (550,000 b/d) — limited domestic refining capacity; imports primarily from Gulf refineries; emergence timeline potentially faster for nations with no strategic reserves
- **Combined severity is multiplicative, not additive:** Fuel shortage degrades logistics and distribution capacity at exactly the moment goods shortages require adaptive responses
- **Most exposed countries:** Kenya, Tanzania, Uganda, Ethiopia
- **Also affected:** Diaspora remittance flows disrupted; air freight (cut flowers, fresh produce exports via DXB) disrupted immediately
- See: [consumer_goods_supply_chain.md](consumer_goods_supply_chain.md)
